Season Journey

Weeks 1–5

Injury Chaos Begins

Top draft picks sidelined early. Thanks to the strong roster dominating FG%, REB, AST, and BLK categories, I reached rank 2 early.

Weeks 6–18

Multiple Injuries, Holding Position

Roster never fully healthy for 18 straight weeks. Utilized strategic waiver wire moves on category-specific strengths of REB, AST, and low TOs to maintain standings at rank 9.

Week 19 — Turning Point

🔑 Player come back + KD + Raynaud Trade

4 players come back from injury after All-star break, and acquired Kevin Durant and Maxime Raynaud at low cost thanks to season ending sale. Projection model flagged an immediate boost across 3PT, PTS, STL.

Weeks 19–21

Roster at Full Strength

Jokić, Durant, and Paul George all healthy simultaneously for the first time. Weekly win probabilities climbed above 85%.

Week 22 — The Tank

🎲 Strategic Seed 8 Decision

Identified that Jokić and Clingan had only 2 games in Week 23 due to NBA scheduling. Deliberately lost Week 21 to drop to Seed 8, avoiding a top-seed semi-final during the weakest week.

Weeks 23–24

🏆 Playoff Dominance

Semi-finals won against a mid-seed opponent despite Jokić's limited games. Finals entered with full roster and 91.7% simulated win probability.

The Technical Stack

🤖

Yahoo API Automation

Daily automated pulls of roster stats, injury flags, and matchup data via OAuth. Output pushed directly to Excel reports each morning.

🎲

Monte Carlo Simulation

100,000 simulations per matchup using Poisson distributions per player per game. Generated category-level and overall win probabilities.

📊

Excel Matchup Board

Auto-generated weekly Excel boards showing projected vs. actual stats, games remaining, injury status, and category breakdowns.

🏥

Injury Tracking

ESPN injury feed integrated with roster data. Automated GTD/OUT flags with season-average fallbacks to keep projections realistic.

⚗️

Last-30 Baseline Stats

Rolling 30-day averages as projection baseline, with season-stat fallback for players returning from injury.

🌲

Games Remaining Model

NBA schedule scraper estimated remaining games per team each week, driving volume-adjusted projections that raw averages miss.

The Strategic Tank

My two best players had a critical NBA scheduling problem in Week 23:

  • Nikola Jokić — elite across all 7 categories, but only 2 games in Week 23
  • Donovan Clingan — elite REB + BLK, but only 2 games in Week 23

This made Week 23 my weakest week of the playoffs regardless of seeding. The key question was: which opponent do I face in that weak week?

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import matplotlib.patches as mpatches
import matplotlib.gridspec as gridspec

fig = plt.figure(figsize=(13, 6.5))
gs = gridspec.GridSpec(2, 1, hspace=0.7)

xs = [0.3, 4.2, 8.1]
week_labels = ['Week 22\n(Quarter-finals)', 'Week 23\n(Semi-finals)', 'Week 24\n(Finals)']
BOX_W, BOX_H = 3.5, 0.8

def draw_row(ax, title, title_color, boxes):
    ax.set_xlim(0, 12.5)
    ax.set_ylim(-0.05, 1.6)
    ax.axis('off')
    ax.text(0.2, 1.52, title, color=title_color,
            fontsize=10.5, fontweight='bold', va='top')

    for i, (label, sub, color) in enumerate(boxes):
        x = xs[i]
        rect = mpatches.FancyBboxPatch(
            (x, 0.45), BOX_W, BOX_H,
            boxstyle="round,pad=0.1",
            linewidth=1.3, edgecolor='#bbbbbb',
            facecolor=color
        )
        ax.add_patch(rect)
        ax.text(x + BOX_W/2,
                0.45 + BOX_H/2 + (0.1 if sub else 0),
                label, ha='center', va='center',
                color='white', fontsize=9, fontweight='bold')
        if sub:
            ax.text(x + BOX_W/2,
                    0.45 + BOX_H/2 - 0.18,
                    sub, ha='center', va='center',
                    color='#eeeeee', fontsize=7.8)
        ax.text(x + BOX_W/2, 0.38, week_labels[i],
                ha='center', va='top',
                color='#666666', fontsize=7.5)
        if i < len(boxes) - 1:
            ax.annotate('',
                xy=(xs[i+1] - 0.05, 0.45 + BOX_H/2),
                xytext=(x + BOX_W + 0.05, 0.45 + BOX_H/2),
                arrowprops=dict(arrowstyle='->', color='#888888', lw=1.5))

# PATH A: Seed 3-6
ax1 = fig.add_subplot(gs[0])
draw_row(ax1,
    title='PATH A — Seed 3 to 6  (rejected)',
    title_color='#c62828',
    boxes=[
        ('QF: vs Seed 7/8',    '✅ Jokić 4G — Win easily',       '#2e7d32'),
        ('SF: vs Seed 1 or 2', '❌ Jokić only 2G — Cannot win',  '#c62828'),
        ('Finals: if reached', '⚠️ Never gets here',             '#9e9e9e'),
    ]
)
ax1.text(12.3, 0.85, '~8%\nchamp\nprob',
         ha='right', va='center', fontsize=8.5,
         color='#c62828', fontweight='bold')

# PATH B: Seed 8
ax2 = fig.add_subplot(gs[1])
draw_row(ax2,
    title='PATH B — Seed 8  ✓ chosen',
    title_color='#2e7d32',
    boxes=[
        ('QF: vs Seed 1',      '⚠️ Jokić 4G — 50/50',           '#e65100'),
        ('SF: vs Seed 4 or 5', '✅ Jokić 2G — Still winnable',   '#1565c0'),
        ('Finals: Week 24',    '✅ Full roster — 91.7% win prob', '#1b5e20'),
    ]
)
ax2.text(12.3, 0.85, '~16%\nchamp\nprob',
         ha='right', va='center', fontsize=8.5,
         color='#2e7d32', fontweight='bold')

fig.suptitle(
    "The Tank Decision: Jokić's Week 23 Schedule Determined Everything",

)

Key Takeaways

TipWhat Made the Difference

  1. Baseline quality — Last-30-day stats with injury-aware fallbacks gave realistic projections, not season averages skewed by early-season performance.

  2. Games remaining model — Knowing a player has 4 games vs 2 games this week often matters more than per-game averages alone.

  3. Simulation over gut feel — Monte Carlo revealed 3PM/TO were already lost before the week started, so focus shifted to winning the other 5 categories.

  4. The tank was counter-intuitive — Conventional wisdom says chase the highest seed. The scheduling data showed the opposite was optimal.
